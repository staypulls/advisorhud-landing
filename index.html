The Operational Reality

Multi-Custodian RIA Ops
Is Harder Than It Should Be.

Proxy operates across multiple custodians, manages dozens of active advisors, and runs a high volume of concurrent service requests — with no unified system to track any of it.

✕ Visibility Gap
No Single View of What's Active

Onboarding status lives in emails and memory. Service requests are scattered across inboxes. There's no reliable way to know what's open, what's stuck, or what's at risk — without asking.

✕ Ownership Ambiguity
Accountability Breaks Down at Scale

Tasks get assigned verbally or in passing. Without a system of record, ownership is assumed rather than confirmed — and things fall through the cracks on busy days.

✕ Exception Blindness
Problems Surface After the Fact

By the time an overdue item becomes visible, it's already an advisor escalation or a client impact. The team is reactive when it needs to be proactive.

✕ Custodian Fragmentation
Every Custodian Is a Different Context Switch

Fidelity, Schwab, Pershing, AssetMark, Cambridge — each has its own portal, its own process, its own quirks. There's no unified workflow layer sitting above all of them.

What AdvisorHUD Gives Operations

One System.
Everything Visible.

AdvisorHUD was designed around the actual workflow of a multi-custodian RIA ops team — not adapted from generic project management software. Every module reflects how Proxy's back-office actually operates.

Onboarding
End-to-End Onboarding Visibility

Every active advisor onboarding is tracked from week 1 registration through full platform access — with progress, task completion, deadlines, and blockers visible at a glance.

Requests
Centralized Request Queue

Every service request — account openings, ACATs, paperwork, access setup — surfaces in a single prioritized queue with SLA age, assigned owner, and current status.

Alerts
Exception & SLA Alert System

Overdue tasks, blocked items, and SLA breaches are surfaced automatically. The ops team sees problems before they become advisor escalations or client impacts.

Team
Ops Workload Intelligence

A live view of what each team member has open. Leadership can see where load is concentrated, where capacity exists, and where bottlenecks are forming — in real time.

Custodians
Multi-Custodian Workflow Layer

Built for firms running Fidelity/NFS, Schwab, Pershing, AssetMark, and Cambridge/WealthPort — with workflows that reflect custodian-specific processes and requirements.

Clarity
Advisor vs. Internal Separation

Internal ops notes, flags, and communications stay internal. Advisor-facing status and updates are clean and appropriate — managed within the same system, not a separate one.

Why This Exists

Built on a Simple Premise.
Ops Deserves Better Tooling.

AdvisorHUD started as a question: what would RIA operations look like if the team had the same situational awareness that a pilot has in the cockpit — everything critical, visible, surfaced before it becomes a problem. These are the principles it's built around.

Principle 01
Visibility Before Action

You can't manage what you can't see. Before anything else, AdvisorHUD is designed to give operations a complete, accurate picture of everything in motion — onboardings, requests, alerts, workload — at any moment.

Principle 02
Exceptions Should Find You

A good ops system doesn't wait to be checked. It surfaces problems proactively — flagging overdue items, SLA risk, and blocked tasks before they require escalation or become advisor-visible issues.

Principle 03
Ownership Must Be Unambiguous

Every task, every request, every open item has a named owner and a clear status. Not assumed. Not implied. Explicitly assigned and tracked — so accountability is built into the system, not left to culture.
